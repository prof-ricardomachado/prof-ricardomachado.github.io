Solução 3.5.4.1.

Usando a regra da cadeia (Caso II):
\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial f}{\partial r} = \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} \cdot \frac{\partial x}{\partial r} + \frac{\partial f}{\partial y} \cdot \frac{\partial y}{\partial r}\text{.} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial f}{\partial r} = (2xy - 2x)(\cos \theta) + (x^2 + 2y)(\sin \theta)\text{.} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial f}{\partial \theta} = \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} \cdot \frac{\partial x}{\partial \theta} + \frac{\partial f}{\partial y} \cdot \frac{\partial y}{\partial \theta}\text{.} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial f}{\partial \theta} = (2xy - 2x)(-r \sin \theta) + (x^2 + 2y)(r \cos \theta)\text{.} \end{equation*}
