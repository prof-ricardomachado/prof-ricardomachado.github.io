Teorema 3.5.2. Regra da Cadeia - Caso II.

Sob as devidas condições de diferenciabilidade, as derivadas parciais da função composta \(h(x,y)\) são:
\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial h}{\partial x} = \frac{\partial f}{\partial u} \cdot \frac{\partial u}{\partial x} + \frac{\partial f}{\partial v} \cdot \frac{\partial v}{\partial x} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial h}{\partial y} = \frac{\partial f}{\partial u} \cdot \frac{\partial u}{\partial y} + \frac{\partial f}{\partial v} \cdot \frac{\partial v}{\partial y}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Essas equações podem ser reescritas de forma compacta em uma forma matricial:
\begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial h}{\partial x} \amp \frac{\partial h}{\partial y} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial f}{\partial u} \amp \frac{\partial f}{\partial v} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial u}{\partial x} \amp \frac{\partial u}{\partial y} \\ \frac{\partial v}{\partial x} \amp \frac{\partial v}{\partial y} \end{bmatrix}\text{.} \end{equation*}
