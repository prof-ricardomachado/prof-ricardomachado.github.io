Exercício 14.

Sejam \(R, S\) e \(T\) transformações lineares de \(\mathbb{R}^3\) em \(\mathbb{R}^3\text{.}\) Considere a base canônica do \(\mathbb{R}^3, \mathcal{C}=\{(1, 0, 0), (0, 1, 0), (0, 0, 1)\}\text{.}\) Se
\begin{equation*} [R]_{\mathcal{C}}^{\mathcal{C}}=\left[\begin{array}{rrr} 1 \amp 0 \amp 1 \\ 2 \amp 1 \amp 1 \\ 0 \amp -1 \amp 1 \end{array} \right] \text{ e } [S]_{\mathcal{C}}^{\mathcal{C}} = \left[\begin{array}{rrr} -2 \amp 1 \amp -1 \\ 3 \amp 1 \amp 2 \\ 1 \amp -2 \amp 0 \end{array} \right], \end{equation*}
determine \(T\) tal que \(R=S\circ T\text{.}\)
