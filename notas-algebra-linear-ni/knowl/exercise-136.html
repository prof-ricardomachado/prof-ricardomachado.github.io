Exercício 15.

Se \(R(x, y)=(2x, x-y, y)\) e \(S(x, y, z)=(y-z, z-x)\text{.}\) Considere \(\mathcal{C}_2\) e \(\mathcal{C}_3\) são as bases canônicas de \(\mathbb{R}^2\) e \(\mathbb{R}^3\) respectivamente, determine:
  1. (i) \([R\circ S]_{\mathcal{C}_3}^{\mathcal{C}_3}\text{;}\)
  2. (ii) \([S\circ R]_{\mathcal{C}_2}^{\mathcal{C}_2}\text{;}\)
  3. (iii) \(R\circ S\) e \(S\circ R\) explicitamente.
