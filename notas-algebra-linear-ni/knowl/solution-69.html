Solução 6.6.4.1.

Sendo \(\mathbf{A} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ -1 & 1 \end{pmatrix}\text{,}\) então
\begin{align*} \mathbf{A}^2 = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 4 \\ -2 & -1 \end{pmatrix}. \end{align*}
Logo,
\begin{align*} g(\mathbf{A}) &= \mathbf{A}^2 -2\mathbf{A} + 3\mathbf{I}\\ &= \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 4 \\ -2 & -1 \end{pmatrix} - 2\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ -1 & 1 \end{pmatrix} + 3\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix}\\ &= \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix}. \end{align*}
