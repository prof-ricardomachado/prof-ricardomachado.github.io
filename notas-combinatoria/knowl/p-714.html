item e) Os termos de \((3x+5)^{13}\) com expoentes ímpares são os termos \(T_1, T_3, \ldots, T_{13}\text{.}\) Note que

\begin{gather*} (3x+5)^{13} = T_1 + T_2 + \cdots +T_{13} + T_{14} \\ (3x-5)^{13} = T_1 - T_2 + \cdots +T_{13} - T_{14} \end{gather*}

portanto

\begin{equation*} q(x) = \frac{(3x+5)^{13}+(3x-5)^{13}}{2} = T_1+ T_3 + \cdots + T_{13}. \end{equation*}

Então, basta calcular \(q(1) = \frac{8^{13} + (-2)^{13}}{2} = 2^{38}-2^{12}\text{.}\)
