Solução 3.4.9.1.

Como \(\text{arcsen}(x)\) é contínua e é a inversa de \(f(x) = \text{sen}(x)\) em \(x\in \left[ -\frac{\pi}{2}, \frac{\pi}{2} \right]\text{,}\) temos

\begin{equation} \text{arcsen}'(x) = \frac{1}{f'(\text{arcsen}(x))} = \frac{1}{\cos(\text{arcsen}(x))}\tag{3.4.1} \end{equation}

pois, \(f'(x) = \cos(x)\text{.}\) Sabemos que

\begin{equation*} \cos(a)^2 + \text{sen}(a)^2 = 1 \end{equation*}

Para \(a = \text{arcsen}(x)\text{,}\) temos

\begin{equation*} \cos^2(\text{arcsen}(x)) + \underbrace{\text{sen}^2(\text{arcsen}(x))}_{x^2} = 1 \end{equation*}

segue

\begin{equation} \cos^2(\text{arcsen}(x)) = 1 -x^2 \Rightarrow \cos(\text{arcsen}(x)) = \sqrt{1 -x^2} \tag{3.4.2} \end{equation}

pois \(\text{arcsen}(x)\in \left[ -\frac{\pi}{2}, \frac{\pi}{2} \right]\text{.}\) Substituindo (3.4.2) em (3.4.1) temos

\begin{equation*} \text{arcsen}'(x) = \frac{1}{\cos(\text{arcsen}(x))} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}, ~~~ -1\lt x\lt1. \end{equation*}
