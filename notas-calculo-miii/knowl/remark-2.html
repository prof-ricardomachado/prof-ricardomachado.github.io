Nota 1.1.4.

Podemos representar \(f:I\rightarrow \mathbb{R}^2\) por
\begin{equation*} f(t)= (f_1(t), f_2(t)) \quad \text{ ou } \quad f(t)= f_1(t)\vec{i}+ f_2(t)\vec{j}, \end{equation*}
com \(\vec{i}=(1,0), \vec{j}=(0,1).\)
JÃ¡ \(f:I\rightarrow \mathbb{R}^3\) pode ser representada por
\begin{equation*} f(t)= (f_1(t), f_2(t), f_3(t)) \quad \text{ ou } \quad f(t)= f_1(t)\vec{i}+ f_2(t)\vec{j} + f_3(t)\vec{k}, \end{equation*}
com \(\vec{i}=(1,0,0), \vec{j}=(0,1,0)\) e \(\vec{k}=(0,0,1)\text{.}\)
