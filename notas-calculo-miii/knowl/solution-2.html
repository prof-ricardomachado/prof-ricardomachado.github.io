Solução 1.1.17.1.

Note que
  1. \(g_1(t)= t^2,~ D(g_1)= \mathbb{R}\text{;}\)
  2. \(\displaystyle g_2(t) =\ln(t+2),~ D(g_2)= \{t\in \mathbb{R}; t+2>0\}= (-2,+\infty);\)
  3. \(\displaystyle g_3(t)= \sqrt{t+1},~ D(g_3)= \{t\in\mathbb{R}; t+1\geq 0\}= [-1, +\infty).\)
Portanto,
\begin{equation*} D(g) = D(g_1)\cap D(g_2)\cap D(g_3)= \mathbb{R}\cap (-2,+\infty)\cap [-1,+\infty) = [-1,+\infty). \end{equation*}
