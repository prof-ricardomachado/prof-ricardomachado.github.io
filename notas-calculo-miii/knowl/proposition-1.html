Proposição 1.2.1.

Sejam \(f: I \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^3, ~g: I \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^3\) funções vetoriais tais que
\begin{equation*} f(t)= (f_1(t), f_2(t), f_3(t)), \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} g(t)= (g_1(t), g_2(t), g_3(t)) \end{equation*}
e \(\lambda: I\rightarrow \mathbb{R}\) uma função real, então
  1. \(f+ g:I\rightarrow \mathbb{R}^3\) está bem definida e
    \begin{equation*} (f+ g)(t)= (f_1(t) + g_1(t), f_2(t)+ g_2(t), f_3(t)+ g_3(t)); \end{equation*}
  2. \(\lambda f:I \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^3\) está bem definida e
    \begin{equation*} (\lambda f)(t)= (\lambda(t)f_1(t), \lambda(t)f_2(t), \lambda(t)f_3(t)); \end{equation*}
  3. \(f\cdot g: I\rightarrow\mathbb{R}\) está bem definida e
    \begin{equation*} (f\cdot g)(t)= f_1(t) g_1(t)+ f_2(t) g_2(t)+ f_3(t) g_3(t); \end{equation*}
  4. \(f\times g: I \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^3\) está bem definida e
    \begin{equation*} (f\times g)(t)= \left|\begin{array}{ccc} \vec{i}\amp\vec{j}\amp\vec{k}\\ f_1(t)\amp f_2(t)\amp f_3(t)\\ g_1(t)\amp g_2(t)\amp g_3(t) \end{array}\right|. \end{equation*}
